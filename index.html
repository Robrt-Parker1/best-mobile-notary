What is a Notary Public and What Do They Do?

A Notary Public in Shawnee KS plays a vital role in ensuring the authenticity and legality of important documents. Whether you need to notarize a power of attorney, real estate transaction, or business agreement, a notary public serves as an impartial witness to deter fraud. Their services help individuals and businesses validate signatures and confirm the identity of involved parties.

The Role of a Notary Public

A notary public is a state-appointed official authorized to perform various legal functions, including witnessing signatures and administering oaths. Their primary duty is to verify the identity of signers and ensure they are signing documents willingly and without coercion. Notaries help maintain the integrity of legal transactions, making them indispensable in various industries.

How to Become a Notary Public

Becoming a notary public requires meeting specific state requirements, which may include training, exams, and background checks. In Kansas, applicants must be at least 18 years old, complete an application process, and obtain a notary bond. Once commissioned, a notary public can begin performing official duties, ensuring the authenticity of documents for residents and businesses.

The Importance of Notarizing a Power of Attorney

A power of attorney is a legal document that grants one person the authority to act on behalf of another. Notarizing this document is crucial to ensure its validity and acceptance by financial institutions and government agencies. A notary public verifies the identity of the person granting power and ensures they are making the decision voluntarily.
